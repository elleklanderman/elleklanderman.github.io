Is it More Beneficial to Have a Strong Defense or a Strong Offense to Win Football Championships?

nfl
football
college
Author

Elle Klanderman

Published

April 14, 2025

The classic saying “defense wins championships” has been a part of football history for decades. But with the constantly changing world of college football and the NFL, some fans have started to wonder if a powerful offense is actually more important in winning titles today.

To answer this question, let’s look at some data.

Sports Reference provides season data for every NFL and college football team. For this, we’ll be looking at the last 10 seasons. First, let’s take a look at the points scored (offense) and the points allowed (defense) from teams that have played in the last 10 Super Bowls.

This chart compares turnovers (red dots) and takeaways (green dots) for each of the teams in the last 10 Super Bowls. The lines between them represent the teams turnover margin, with the black lines being the teams that won the Super Bowl that year.

You can clearly see that almost all of the Super Bowl teams had more takeaways than turnovers. Turnovers are usually the offense’s fault, they are caused by things like interceptions, fumbles, and overall bad decision-making by the offense. Takeaways are all about the defense. They usually happen when the defense is strong, they force mistakes, make big plays, and put the pressure on the offense, causing them to lose possession of the ball.

Turnovers are a critical part of the game, and usually the team that wins the turnover battle wins the game, so the defenses that can successfully take the ball away from their opponents play a very important role in who wins the game.

This table compares defensive and offensive yards per play. Defensively, teams want to have low yards per play, meaning that they stop their opponents from moving the ball forward. The darker the red in the chart, the worse the team’s defense was. Offensively is the opposite. Teams want to have as many yards per play as possible, so they can get to the endzone faster. The darker the green in the chart, the better the team’s offense was.

What stands out in this chart is that almost every championship team was strong on at least one side of the ball. Teams like Alabama in 2020 and LSU in 2019 were very strong offensively, but still had respectable defenses. Others, like Georgia in 2021 or Alabama in 2017, were strong on both sides of the ball. The teams that were weaker defensively, like Ohio State in 2020 or Washington in 2023, made it to the championship, but couldn’t win. This shows that an efficient defense can make up for a sub-par offense, but it is much harder to win with a weak defense.
